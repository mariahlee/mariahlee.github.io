About Me

I am a computational biology student who loves the discovery aspect of research. While wet lab work generates vast amounts of data, I aspire to be the one making sense of it all. My research interests include using deep learning to uncover the functions of noncoding regions, particularly noncoding RNAs. I believe we need as much information about the body as possible to truly acheive personalized medicine, so I will do my part of increasing our knowledge in the hopes that my discoveries will lead to other breakthroughs.

Mission Statement

I aim to create and utilize new technologies to advance our knowledge of noncoding regions, with the goal of uncovering mechanisms that drive health and disease. Through collaboration and mentorship, I aspire to inspire and lead others, especially those underrepresented in genomics, to push the boundaries of scientific discovery.
